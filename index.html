Note: UK statutory holiday entitlement is 5.6 weeks (28 days for full-time workers), which employers may count bank holidays toward. Scotland observes 9 bank holidays; Northern Ireland observes 10. The UK does not have a single national "public holiday" law — bank holidays are set by Royal Proclamation or statute.

Note: Portugal has 13 national public holidays, among the highest in Europe. The Labour Code (Código do Trabalho) mandates a minimum of 22 working days of paid annual leave. Good Friday, Easter Sunday, and Corpus Christi are moveable feasts that shift each year. Some municipalities also observe a local patron saint's day as an additional holiday.
